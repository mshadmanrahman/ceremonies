# LinkedIn Post — Ceremonies.dev Launch

---

Stop running retros where nobody remembers what they committed to last time.

I've sat through hundreds of retrospectives. Here's the pattern:

Team writes action items.
Team nods.
Sprint starts.
Action items vanish into Confluence.
Two weeks later: "What did we agree to fix?"

Nobody knows. Fresh start. Again.

So I built something to fix that.

Ceremonies.dev opens every retro with "The Haunting." Your previous action items appear on screen. Done or not done. No skipping. The unfinished work comes back.

A few other opinions baked in:

- True anonymity during writing. No typing indicators, no avatars, no "I can see who's typing right now" social pressure
- Enforced phases. Can't skip grouping. Can't jump to voting. The research says these phases matter, so the tool enforces them
- Modified Fibonacci with 4. The 3-to-5 gap is where estimation arguments live. Adding 4 closes it
- Zero auth for participants. Room code. Join. Done. No account needed

It's free. It's open source. Participants don't need accounts.

We just shipped Jira integration too. Auto-complete tickets in estimation, sync story points back automatically. The kind of thing that actually closes the feedback loop.
