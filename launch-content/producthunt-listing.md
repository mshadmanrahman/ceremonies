# Product Hunt Listing — Ceremonies

## Tagline (60 chars max)
The agile tool that haunts you with last sprint's commitments

## Description

Ceremonies is a free, open-source agile ceremony tool for teams that are tired of retros where nobody remembers what they agreed to fix.

**The Haunting:** Every retro opens by confronting your previous action items. Done or not done. No skipping. The unfinished work comes back.

**True anonymity:** No typing indicators, no avatars, no presence hints during writing. Your team writes what they actually think.

**Enforced phases:** Writing, grouping, voting, discussing. The tool enforces the structure so facilitators don't have to.

**Estimation:** Modified Fibonacci with 4 (closes the 3-to-5 argument gap). Simultaneous reveal. Session history.

**Jira integration:** Auto-complete tickets, sync story points back automatically.

**Zero friction:** Participants join with a room code. No account needed. Works in the browser on any device.

Free plan: 10 saved sessions. Pro plan for unlimited. Self-host if you want.

## First Comment (from maker)

Hey PH! I'm Shadman, a PM who got frustrated enough with retro tools to build one.

The core insight: every retro tool I've used gives you sticky notes and hopes you'll follow the process. None of them remember what your team committed to last time. None of them enforce the phases that prevent anchoring bias and groupthink.

So I built Ceremonies. It has opinions. The Haunting (Phase 0) forces teams to confront last sprint's action items before writing new cards. It's uncomfortable on purpose.

Stack: Next.js, PartyKit (real-time WebSockets), Clerk, Neon Postgres. Open source MIT.

I'd love to hear what ceremonies or rituals your team uses that existing tools don't support well. What's missing?
