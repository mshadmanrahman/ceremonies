## Tweet 2 (The Problem)
Here's the retro loop every team runs:

Write action items.
Nod.
Sprint starts.
Action items vanish.
Two weeks later: "What did we agree to fix?"

Nobody knows. Fresh start. Again.

## Tweet 3 (The Haunting)
So ceremonies.dev opens every retro with "The Haunting."

Your previous action items appear on screen. Done or not done?

No skipping. The unfinished work comes back.

It's uncomfortable. That's the point.

## Tweet 4 (True Anonymity)
Most "anonymous" retro tools: your name is hidden but everyone can see who's typing.

Ceremonies: no typing indicators. No avatars. No presence hints during writing.

You write what you actually think.

## Tweet 5 (Estimation)
Estimation room with modified Fibonacci:

1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 8, 13, ?, coffee

That 4 matters. The 3-to-5 gap is where teams waste the most time arguing.

Adding 4 closes it.

## Tweet 6 (Zero Friction)
Participants join with a room code. No account. No email. No friction.

Only facilitators need to sign in.

Works in the browser. Any device.
